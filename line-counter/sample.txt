Franz Kafka was a writer born in Prague in 1883.
He is known for works like The Metamorphosis and The Trial.

Kafka’s writing often explores themes of alienation, bureaucracy, and absurdity.
His characters frequently face systems they cannot understand or control.

The term "Kafkaesque" is used to describe situations that are surreal, complex, or nightmarishly bureaucratic.
Kafka worked in an insurance company while writing in his spare time.

He died in 1924, leaving much of his work unfinished.
